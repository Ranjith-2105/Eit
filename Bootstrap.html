Royal Enfield, a historic motorcycle manufacturer, originated in 1893 as a bicycle company in England. They produced their first motorcycle in 1901 and gained prominence during both World Wars for military use. The iconic Bullet model emerged, symbolizing durability. Post-war, Royal Enfield faced challenges in the UK, leading to its closure in 1971. However, its Indian subsidiary, Enfield India, continued production and saw a revival in the 1990s under the Eicher Group. Modernizing while retaining classic design elements, Royal Enfield regained global popularity, especially with models like the Classic series and the Interceptor 650. Today, the brand is renowned for its retro-styled motorcycles and maintains a dedicated global fan base.
